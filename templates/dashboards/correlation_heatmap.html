Pearson r (Correlation Coefficient)
A measure of linear association between two variables. Ranges from −1 (perfect negative) to +1 (perfect positive). r = 0 means no linear relationship.
Strong Correlation
|r| ≥ 0.5. Indicates a meaningful linear relationship that may warrant investigation.
Positive Correlation
r > 0: as one metric increases, the other tends to increase.
Negative Correlation
r < 0: as one metric increases, the other tends to decrease.
Collinearity Warning
Very high correlations (|r| > 0.9) between different metrics may indicate they measure the same underlying phenomenon. Treat them as complementary, not additive.
Causation
Correlation does not imply causation. A high r value shows that two metrics move together historically, but does not prove that one causes the other.
