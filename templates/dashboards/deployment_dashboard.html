DORA Metrics
DevOps Research and Assessment (DORA) metrics are industry-standard measures of software delivery performance. These metrics help teams understand their deployment velocity, stability, and efficiency. Research shows that high-performing teams excel across all four DORA metrics.
Deployment Frequency
How often code is successfully deployed to production. Measured as deployments per week. Higher is better - more frequent deployments indicate better agility and faster delivery of value to customers.

Elite performers: Multiple deployments per day
High performers: Daily to weekly deployments
Medium performers: Weekly to monthly deployments
Build Success Rate
Percentage of builds that complete successfully without failures. Higher is better - indicates code quality, test coverage, and build stability.

A high success rate (>90%) suggests robust testing and quality gates. Low success rates can indicate flaky tests, infrastructure issues, or code quality problems.
Build Duration (Median)
The median time it takes to complete a build, measured in minutes. Lower is better - faster builds mean quicker feedback for developers.

We show both median (typical time) and P85 (85th percentile) to account for variability. Long build times can slow down development velocity and delay feedback on code changes.
Lead Time for Changes
The time from when code is committed to when it's successfully deployed to production, measured in hours. Lower is better - shorter lead times mean faster response to customer needs and market changes.

Elite performers: Less than 1 hour
High performers: 1 day to 1 week
Medium performers: 1 week to 1 month

This metric combines build time, test time, approval processes, and deployment time.
P85 (85th Percentile)
A statistical measure showing the value below which 85% of observations fall. This helps filter out extreme outliers while capturing realistic upper bounds.

For example, if P85 build duration is 15 minutes, it means 85% of builds complete in 15 minutes or less.

üìä Data Collection Details

Time Period
90-day lookback period for all deployment metrics. This provides enough data for meaningful statistics while remaining relevant to current performance.
Build Criteria
Only completed builds (succeeded or failed) are counted. Canceled or in-progress builds are excluded from metrics. Deployment frequency counts only successful builds.
Why These Metrics Matter
DORA metrics correlate strongly with organizational performance and business outcomes. Teams that excel in these metrics deliver more value, respond faster to incidents, and have higher job satisfaction. These are leading indicators of overall engineering effectiveness.
